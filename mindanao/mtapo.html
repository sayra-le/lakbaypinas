WHY VISIT
MT. APO

Mt. Apo is the tallest peak in the Philippines, rising at 2,954 meters above sea level. It is a dormant stratovolcano and is often referred to as the “King of Philippine Mountains.” The summit offers breathtaking views of nearby mountain ranges, forests, and volcanic landscapes.

The mountain is covered with lush forests, home to various endemic species like the Philippine eagle. Its slopes feature mossy woods, sulfur vents, and scenic lakes that create a rich hiking experience. It is part of the Mount Apo Natural Park, a protected area preserving biodiversity.

Mt. Apo is also a sacred place for the indigenous tribes in Mindanao. Locals consider the mountain spiritual and often hold rituals in its honor. This cultural importance adds depth to every visitor's journey to the peak.

Activities Your Must Try

Trekking to the Summit
Conquer the highest point in the Philippines by trekking up Mt. Apo's challenging trails. The climb typically takes 2–4 days depending on the route. Along the way, hikers witness breathtaking views and diverse wildlife.
Camping Under the Stars
Tourists can set up tents at designated campsites near the summit. At night, the mountain sky is clear, making it perfect for stargazing. The cool breeze and peaceful environment provide a refreshing escape from city life.
Hot Spring Relaxation
After a long hike, relax in the natural hot springs near Lake Agco. These warm geothermal pools soothe tired muscles and offer a relaxing experience. Surrounded by nature, it's a favorite recovery spot for trekkers.
Wildlife and Bird Watching
Mt. Apo is a haven for bird watchers and nature lovers. Spot rare birds like the majestic Philippine eagle in their natural habitat. The mountain's rich ecosystem makes it ideal for nature photography and wildlife observation.
