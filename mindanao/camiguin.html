CAMIGUIN ISLAND


Volcanic Island in the Philippines.

Camiguin Island is known as the "Island Born of Fire" due to its volcanic origin.

It features white sand beaches, waterfalls, hot and cold springs, and historical sites.

The island is peaceful, scenic, and ideal for nature lovers and adventurers.

WHY VISIT
CAMIGUIN ISLAND

Camiguin Island is a small but captivating destination in Northern Mindanao, famous for its volcanoes and natural hot springs. The island was formed through volcanic eruptions, earning it the nickname “Island Born of Fire.” Despite its size, Camiguin boasts rich biodiversity and stunning natural landscapes.

Tourists can explore both land and sea, from hiking volcanoes to snorkeling in crystal-clear waters. One popular spot is the Sunken Cemetery, a historic site submerged due to a volcanic eruption. Visitors also enjoy the White Island sandbar, which offers breathtaking views of the mountains and sea.

Camiguin also hosts cultural festivals such as the Lanzones Festival, celebrating the sweet tropical fruit. The island is accessible by ferry or flight, and is known for its friendly locals and laid-back vibe. It’s the perfect getaway for those who seek nature, culture, and serenity.

Activities Your Must Try

Visit the White Island Sandbar
A pristine, powdery white sandbar just a boat ride away from Mambajao.
Snorkel at the Sunken Cemetery
Dive or snorkel to explore the remains of a town buried by a volcanic eruption.
Hike Mount Hibok-Hibok
A challenging but rewarding trek offering panoramic views of the island.
Dip in Ardent Hot Springs
A relaxing geothermal pool surrounded by lush greenery, perfect for unwinding.
