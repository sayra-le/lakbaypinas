ISLAND GARDEN CITY OF SAMAL


Tropical Island City in Davao del Norte in the Philippines.

Samal Island is known for its pristine white sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and peaceful island atmosphere.

It serves as a popular getaway spot for locals and tourists alike, especially those coming from nearby Davao City.

Aside from its natural beauty, the island also hosts luxury resorts, marine sanctuaries, and hidden caves.

WHY VISIT
ISLAND GARDEN CITY OF SAMAL

Island Garden City of Samal is one of the most accessible island destinations in the Philippines due to its proximity to Davao City. The island is composed of Samal and Talikud Islands, offering visitors a mix of beach, adventure, and culture. Despite its growing popularity, it retains a quiet charm that appeals to nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Samal boasts over 70 resorts ranging from budget-friendly to high-end accommodations. Its coastline is dotted with palm-fringed shores, rock formations, and snorkeling areas rich in marine biodiversity. Many locals also earn a living through fishing, tourism, and small-scale farming, making the island vibrant and community-oriented.

Tourism plays a vital role in the local economy, and the government works to keep development sustainable. The island is clean, well-maintained, and promotes eco-tourism through guided nature tours and marine conservation programs. Whether you’re in for relaxation or thrilling activities, Samal has something for everyone.

Island Garden City of Samal Highlights





Activities Your Must Try

Hagimit Falls Adventure
Explore the cascading beauty of Hagimit Falls, a popular natural attraction on the island. Visitors can swim, picnic, and enjoy the refreshing pools surrounded by lush greenery. It’s a great spot for relaxation and nature photography.
Island-Hopping Tour
Talikud Island and nearby coves are perfect for island-hopping excursions. Tourists can snorkel in coral gardens, lounge on sandbars, and discover hidden beaches. These day tours are often bundled with a delicious seafood lunch.
Monfort Bat Sanctuary Visit
See the world’s largest colony of fruit bats at Monfort Bat Sanctuary. This Guinness World Record site houses millions of bats and promotes conservation awareness. It’s an educational and unique stop for eco-tourists.
Scuba Diving & Snorkeling
The island’s underwater world is teeming with life and color, making it ideal for scuba diving and snorkeling. Dive sites such as Coral Garden and Angel’s Cove offer encounters with vibrant reefs, sea turtles, and tropical fish. Certified divers can also explore deeper marine spots with local guides.
