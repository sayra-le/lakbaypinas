INTRAMUROS


Walled City in Manila, Philippines

Intramuros is a historic walled city in the heart of Manila.

It served as the political and religious center during the Spanish colonial era.

Today, it's a popular destination filled with preserved architecture and cultural landmarks.

WHY VISIT
INTRAMUROS

Intramuros, meaning "within the walls," is the oldest district in Manila, built by the Spanish colonizers in the late 1500s. It served as the political, religious, and educational center of the Spanish colonial government. The entire city was fortified with massive stone walls to protect against invasions and attacks.

Inside Intramuros were important structures like government offices, Catholic churches, convents, and schools, including the oldest existing church, San Agustin Church. The streets were narrow and paved with cobblestones, reflecting the classic architecture and layout of a European colonial city. At the heart of it all was Fort Santiago, a key military fortress and prison.

Today, Intramuros is a preserved heritage site where visitors can walk through history. Tourists can explore museums, ride traditional kalesas (horse-drawn carriages), and enjoy cultural performances. It remains a living monument to the Philippines’ rich and complex past.

Intramuros Highlights





Activities Your Must Try

Visit Fort Santiago
Discover this iconic citadel that once served as a military fortress and prison. It features Rizal’s shrine, dungeons, and beautifully preserved gardens.
Tour San Agustin Church and Museum
Enter the oldest stone church in the Philippines, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Inside, you’ll find intricate baroque designs, religious relics, and historical artifacts.
Walk or Bike Around the Walls
Explore the historic stone walls and bastions of Intramuros by foot or bamboo bike (bambike). It’s a great way to appreciate the architecture and get panoramic views of Manila.
Ride a Kalesa (Horse-Drawn Carriage)
Enjoy a traditional kalesa ride through the cobbled streets of Intramuros. It’s a charming way to experience the colonial vibe and learn about the district’s history from local guides.
