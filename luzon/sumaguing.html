SUMAGUING CAVE


Limestone Cave in Sagada, Philippines.

Sumaguing Cave is Sagada’s largest and most popular cave.

It features stunning limestone formations and underground pools.

A thrilling destination for adventure seekers and nature lovers.

WHY VISIT
SUMAGUING CAVE

Sumaguing Cave, often referred to as the "Big Cave," is renowned for its expansive chambers and impressive rock formations. Visitors are captivated by the natural sculptures resembling animals and objects, such as the "King's Curtain" and "Chocolate Cake." The cave's cool atmosphere and intricate pathways offer a unique spelunking experience.

The journey begins with a descent down man-made steps leading to the cave's entrance. Inside, adventurers navigate through slippery rocks, narrow passages, and cold pools of water. Guides assist tourists in exploring safely while sharing fascinating stories about the cave's history and formations.

Sumaguing Cave is a must-visit for those seeking both physical challenge and natural beauty. The combination of adventure and awe-inspiring sights makes it a highlight of any trip to Sagada. Whether you're a seasoned spelunker or a curious traveler, the cave promises an unforgettable experience.

Sumaguing Cave Highlights





Activities Your Must Try

Spelunking Adventure
Embark on a thrilling spelunking journey through Sumaguing Cave, known as the "Big Cave" of Sagada. This adventure involves navigating through narrow passages, climbing over slippery rocks, and wading through cold underground pools.
Admire Stunning Rock Formations
Discover the cave's impressive limestone formations, including stalactites, stalagmites, and unique structures like the "King's Curtain." These natural sculptures have formed over thousands of years, offering a visual feast for visitors.
Cave Connection Tour
For a more challenging adventure, try the Cave Connection Tour, which links Sumaguing Cave to Lumiang Cave. This extended spelunking experience takes about 4-5 hours and offers a deeper exploration of Sagada's underground wonders.
Capture Memorable Moments
Bring a waterproof camera to document your journey through the cave's captivating interiors. From the intricate rock formations to the adventurous paths, there's plenty to capture and remember.
