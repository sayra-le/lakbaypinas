HUNDRED ISLANDS


Island in the Philippines.

Hundred Islands is a group of over 100 beautiful islands and islets in Pangasinan.

Each island has unique features, from white-sand beaches to cliffs and caves.

It's a perfect spot for island hopping, water adventures, and nature sightseeing.

WHY VISIT
HUNDRED ISLANDS

Hundred Islands National Park is a natural treasure of the Philippines, boasting 124 islands at low tide and 123 at high tide. These islands are mostly uninhabited and offer pristine beaches, turquoise waters, and lush greenery. The park is a great destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Some of the popular islands include Governor’s Island, Quezon Island, and Marcos Island. Each one offers a different experience—from swimming and snorkeling to scenic viewpoints and ziplining. Visitors can rent boats to explore these islands and enjoy their serene beauty.

Hundred Islands is also rich in marine biodiversity, making it a favorite for snorkeling and diving. Colorful coral reefs and various fish species thrive in the area, providing a stunning underwater experience. Tourists leave with not only pictures but unforgettable memories of nature's untouched beauty.
