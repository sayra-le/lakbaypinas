WHY VISIT
MAYON VOLCANO

Mayon Volcano is one of the Philippines' most iconic natural landmarks, known for its near-perfect cone shape that captivates both locals and tourists. Towering at 2,462 meters, it stands proudly in the province of Albay, offering breathtaking views from every angle. Its symmetrical beauty has made it a favorite subject for photographs, paintings, and legends passed down through generations.

Despite its elegant appearance, Mayon is an active volcano with a long history of eruptions, some of which have caused significant damage and loss of life. Its activity is closely monitored by volcanologists to ensure the safety of nearby communities. Mayon's powerful presence is a reminder of nature's dual character—both beautiful and unpredictable.

Surrounding Mayon are lush landscapes, fertile plains, and vibrant communities that thrive in its shadow. It is also part of the Mayon Volcano Natural Park, a protected area that attracts hikers, nature lovers, and researchers. The volcano not only shapes the geography of the region but also influences its culture, economy, and the resilience of its people.

Activities Your Must Try

Lava Viewing
Get a close-up view of Mayon's active lava flow, especially after an eruption. You can visit designated safe viewing areas like the Cagsawa Ruins for an incredible perspective of the volcano and its surrounding landscapes.
Trekking and Hiking
For adventurous visitors, hiking to the summit of Mayon is a challenging yet rewarding activity. Various trails, including those around the volcano's base, offer stunning views and a closer look at its diverse flora and fauna.
ATV Rides
Explore the volcanic terrain and the surrounding area on an ATV ride. These guided tours offer an exciting way to experience the rugged landscape and pass through lush forests, lava trails, and valleys.
Visit the Cagsawa Ruins
One of the most famous landmarks near Mayon, the Cagsawa Ruins offers an impressive historical site with an unobstructed view of Mayon. Visitors can take photographs of the iconic bell tower, which was buried during an eruption in the 19th century.
