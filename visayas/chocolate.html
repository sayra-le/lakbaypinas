CHOCOLATE HILLS


Geological formation in the Philippines.

Chocolate Hills consists of over 1,200 cone-shaped hills spread across the Bohol landscape.

The hills turn brown during summer, creating a chocolate-like appearance.

They are a natural wonder and a must-see destination in the Philippines.

WHY VISIT
CHOCOLATE HILLS

The Chocolate Hills are a breathtaking natural formation that stretches across the towns of Carmen, Batuan, and Sagbayan in Bohol. These hills vary in size but are uniformly shaped, making them a geological mystery and visual spectacle. They are made of limestone covered with grass, which turns brown in the dry season.

Declared the Philippines' third National Geological Monument, Chocolate Hills attract thousands of visitors every year. Legends say the hills were formed from the tears of a giant, adding a touch of folklore to their charm. Whether seen from ground level or the viewing deck, the sight is awe-inspiring.

Aside from sightseeing, the area offers a peaceful atmosphere and panoramic views of Bohol’s countryside. Its unique beauty and cultural value make it a symbol of Bohol and Philippine tourism. The site is well-maintained and ideal for both adventure seekers and casual travelers.
